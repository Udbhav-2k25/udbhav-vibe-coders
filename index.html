Functional Mission

Logistics companies lose money and customer trust when packages arrive late. The Supply Chain Oracle acts as a “weather forecast” for shipping, giving managers an early warning of delays and options to fix them before they hit the customer.

Problem

Today, teams usually discover a delay only when:

  • The customer complains
  • The SLA is already broken
  • Operations teams are already firefighting

Oracle’s Promise

Predict delays early, explain why they will happen, and suggest what to do next:

  • Fewer surprises in last-mile delivery
  • Better SLA performance and on-time rate
  • Calmer customers and happier managers

MVP Requirements

The first version focuses on three clear capabilities: prediction, explanation, and recommendation.

MVP · 01

Prediction

Predict ETA and risk level for each shipment using ML.

  • ETA for every active shipment
  • Risk flags: On Track / Medium / High
  • Route-level delay stats for managers
MVP · 02

Explanation

Tell users why a delay is predicted.

  • SHAP values for feature-level impact
  • Top causes: traffic, weather, hub congestion
  • Carrier history and route reliability
MVP · 03

Recommendation

Suggest the best action to reduce delay impact.

  • Faster alternate routes with ETA gain
  • More reliable carriers for that lane
  • Confidence score for each suggestion

AI & ML Engine

The Supply Chain Oracle uses an ensemble of LightGBM and XGBoost models, and SHAP values for explainability at shipment level.

Model Stack

LightGBM + XGBoost Ensemble

Two gradient boosting models are trained on shipment, route and carrier data. Their outputs are blended to get stable predictions.

LightGBM · fast & efficient XGBoost · highly accurate Ensemble · best of both
  • Handles non-linear interactions (route × time × carrier)
  • Scales to thousands of shipments per day
  • Can be retrained regularly as new data arrives
Explainability with SHAP

SHAP Values: “Why is this late?”

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is used on top of the ensemble to explain each prediction in plain language.

Hub congestion score +0.40 (delay)
Weather risk level +0.25 (delay)
Carrier on-time score -0.15 (faster)

Example explanation for a manager: “Heavy congestion at Hub-3 and bad weather on Route A-17 are the main reasons this shipment is likely to be late, even though the carrier is usually reliable.”
